Bug Bite Identification Glossary

Some of the more common bug bites can be found here. Not all bug bites can be found here.

Name: Bug Ref: (image) Bite Ref: (image) Description: Symptoms: Note:
Bedbug Small puffy red bumps that occur in clusters or lines. Often occurring at night in bed. Usually occurring on arms, trunk of the body or neck. Itchy, swelling Can be reoccurring if you don't get rid of them. Get rid of them by regularly vacuuming or steam cleaning carpets and washing clothes and linens in hot water.
Flea Small red bumps occurring in clusters or lines. Usually found around the feet or ankles, but can occur anywhere else. May have distinctive red 'halo'. Symptoms: Itchy, swelling They will be more attracted to your pets. Can be reoccurring if you don't get rid of them. Get rid of them by regularly vacuuming or steam cleaning carpets and washing clothes and linens in hot water. Firstly, deflea your pets.
Mite/Chigger Flat red patches or raised red bumps occuring in clusters or lines. May sometimes develop into pustules or blisters. Typically occurring in ankles, wrists, thighs, groin, and waist. Itchy May have gotten it from walking through wooded or grassy areas. Chiggers do not burrow under skin.
Mosquito Ocurring as single bites on exposed skin. May occur in multiple bites. Small, puffy round bumps or welts. Can be red. Can have a small red dot at its center. Typically becomes hard and swollen after some time has passed. Itchy, swelling Mosquitoes can carry diseases like West Nile and Zika. Consult a physician if you have flu-like symptoms.
Tick Can sometimes leave behind a red spot, but typically stays attached to host until it's done feeding. Mainly found in warmer parts of the body such as groin, armpits and hairline. Painless, mild itchy. May have gotten it from walking through wooded or grassy areas. Ticks can carry lyme disease. Consult with your physician if you suspect you've been bitten by a tick or have developed a bulls-eye rash indicative of lyme disease.
